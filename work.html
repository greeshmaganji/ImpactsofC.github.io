Impact of COVID-19 on Work

The COVID-19 pandemic has impacted students, teachers, researchers, and non-teaching staff in the Education sector. There have also been setbacks for academic centers and learning institutes (Kuhfeld, 2019). The pandemic has not only impacted many people but also introduced them to the new and latest technology.

Job Changes and Remote Work:

The nature of work had substantially changed for people who had sustainable jobs by working remotely. New technologies have been introduced for the connectivity of users across the world. In contrast to the general employees, some groups of employees more easily develop negative perceptions than positive ones, such as autistic employees, teachers, and university employees,due to the challenges of adaptation to teleworking. On top of that, strict management control and monitoring, deteriorated work engagement, and excessive job demand can aggravate employees' mental health and psychological symptoms during remote working (Wei, Yingbo, & Danni, 2021). A study has proven that remote work has been effective and that people in the United States embrace flexibility. More than thirty-five percent of jobs offer full-time work from home, twenty- three percent of employers offer part-time work-from-home flexibility, whereas forty-two percent do not offer work-from-home flexibility as required by the job description (Andr√©, et al., 2022).

Job Outcomes:

The changes in employment will impact many employment outcomes beyond job performance, including organizational commitment, job stress, and job satisfaction. Increasing data suggest that important Organizational changes, particularly those aided by technology, have a big impact on the outcomes of jobs (Venkatesh, 2020). According to another research (Bariso, 2020), jobs having more stress leads to less job satisfaction lead to lower job performance. Companies like Google, Cisco, and Amazon have an extra day off for their employees to relieve their stress and recharge. This method has been effective for employers and employees.
